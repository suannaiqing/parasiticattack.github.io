Demystifying Risks of Model Parasitic Attacks Against Transfer Learning Models



Summary: we demonstrate the risks of MPA-incurred semantic backdoor attacks via two attacks shown in two videos using an in-lab setting. The two attacks (shown in the videos) could result in severe car accidents if they were to actually occur as physical attacks in reality.

Attack Scenario 1

Video 1. The attack maps the class of “Stop Sign” to the class of “Road Number Sign”. The real-time prediction is shown in the top of the left figure, whereas the real-time prediction probabilities for the top-5 classes is shown in the right figure.


Suppose that a traffic sign recognition (TSR) model is backdoored by using an MPA-incurred semantic backdoor attack. The backdoored TSR model is deployed on a self-driving vehicle. This attack maps the “Road Number Sign” class to the “Stop Sign” class. If a self-driving vehicle misclassifies a “Road Number Sign” as a “Stop Sign”, then the vehicle could have a sudden break and stop. Note that a sudden stop on the highway is highly likely to lead to a serious car accident, endangering the lives of the driver and passengers.



Attack Scenario 2

Video 2. The attack maps the class of “Street Light” to the class of “Speed Limit 120km/h Sign”.


In the second attack scenario, the semantic backdoor attack maps the “Street Light” class to the “Speed Limit 120 km/h Sign” class. If the self-driving vehicle misclassifies the “Street Light” class to the “Speed Limit 120 km/h Sign” class, then the vehicle might drive at a risky speed, jeopardizing the lives of the driver and passengers.
